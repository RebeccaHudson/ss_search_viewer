On the front page, it would be helpful to explain the title of the database and state that it works exclusively for humans.

@SNP DB provides statistical evaluation of impact of SNPs on transcription factor-DNA interactions based on position weight matrices (PWM). The current version is built on SNPs from dbSNP Build 144 for the human genome assembly GRCh38/hg38. atSNP DB uses in silico calculations based on the statistical method in Zuo et al., Bioinformatics (2015). atSNP first-time users can start scanning vast amounts of SNP-PWM combinations with two key features:

  • p-values quantifying the impact of SNP on the PWM matches (p-value rank)
  • composite logo plots

@SNP identifies and quantifies best matches to the transcription factor PWMs with both the reference and the SNP alleles in a small window around the SNP location (up to +/- 30 base pairs and considering subsequences spanning the SNP position). It evaluates statistical significance of scores on each allele and calculates statistical significance of the score difference between the reference and the SNP alleles.

The discovery of best matches on the reference and SNP alleles are conducted separately, thus genomic subsequences providing the best matches with both alleles may be different. The change in goodness of match between both alleles indicates impact of SNPs on transcription factor binding ability to DNA. @SNP also provides a composite logo plot for easy visualization of the quality of the reference and SNP allele matches to the PWM and the influence of SNPs on the match change.
